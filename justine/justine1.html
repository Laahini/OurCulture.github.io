Bayanihan Spirit

Bayanihan spirit is a custom and sense of Filipino pride as a community and nation. The word, Bayanihan, is derived from a Filipino word “bayan”, which means nation, town or community. The term bayanihan itself literally means “being in a bayan”, which refers to the spirit of communal unity, work and cooperation to achieve a particular goal.

Justine's Story

Justine and her family live in the Philippines and were one of the families drastically affected by Typhoon Ulysses. The Filipino government attempted to fix the situation but their response was heavily delayed causing local communities bayanihan to take underway. Their bayanihan spirit helped Justine and her family greatly. Her house had been flooded and her family had no shelter and food but local bayanihan spirit helped them with bakeries and stores donating supplies and food.
